# SCQ

Situation. Complication. Question.

## Why this exists

Grounded in the Minto Pyramid Principle (Barbara Minto, McKinsey, 1973). SCQ decomposes problem framing into orthogonal fields: what's true (situation), what's wrong (complication), what to resolve (question), what's fixed (constraints), what's out (non-goals). Each field is independent — knowing one doesn't determine another.

Replaces the old frame output structure (Problem/Motivation/NonGoals/Constraints/OpenQuestions). The improvement: Situation ⊥ Complication is cleaner than Problem ⊥ Motivation (motivation was derivable from problem). Open Questions moved to `#epistemic`.

## Rules

- All five sections present: Situation, Complication, Question, Constraints, Non-goals.
- Situation = stable shared context. Complication = what changed or broke. Don't merge them.
- Question = what must be answered to resolve the complication. Not a list of open questions (that's `#epistemic`).
- Constraints = what can't be changed. Non-goals = what we choose not to address.

## DO NOT

- Merge Situation and Complication into "Problem."
- Derive Complication from Situation — state both explicitly.
- Put open questions / unknowns here — compose `#epistemic` for that.

## Pairs well with

- `#=frame` — primary use case
- `#epistemic` — adds confidence labeling and unknown tracking
- `#factor` — find independent dimensions within the complication
- `#deep` — dig beneath the stated complication to find the real one
